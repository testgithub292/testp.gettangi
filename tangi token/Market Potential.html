The Untapped Potential in the Cannabis Industry: A $33B Market Ready for Disruption


The cannabis industry is one of the most under-served sectors in terms of payment processing, primarily due to the refusal of major banks and payment networks like Visa and MasterCard to service the industry. While cannabis businesses have flourished in recent years due to growing acceptance and legalization in various jurisdictions, the inability to offer compliant and reliable payment solutions has created a massive, untapped opportunity for any provider that can break through the barriers.

Currently, the legal cannabis market in the U.S. alone is valued at approximately $33 billion in annual transactional value. Yet, despite the large sums flowing through this market, cannabis businesses face significant obstacles in their attempts to accept electronic payments. This is a market in desperate need of a compliant, reliable, and transparent payment system that can handle the transactions while keeping businesses within the bounds of legal and regulatory frameworks.



The Payment Struggle: What Banks and Major Payment Providers Won’t Tell You


In the cannabis sector, the inability to access banking services is the root cause of most payment issues. Visa, MasterCard, and other traditional payment networks refuse to provide services to the cannabis industry due to the complexity of regulatory requirements and their fear of running afoul of federal law, even in states where cannabis is legal.

This leaves cannabis businesses with two options: either operate cash-only, or turn to illegitimate, non-compliant payment providers who exploit the gap in the market. The first option—cash-only transactions—creates a series of challenges for cannabis retailers and consumers alike, including:

  • Increased Security Risks: Cash is inherently unsafe and makes businesses more susceptible to theft and robbery.
  • Inefficiency and High Operational Costs: Handling and transporting cash is labor-intensive, costly, and increases the risk of errors.
  • Limited Consumer Reach: Without traditional payment methods, businesses miss out on customers who prefer to use their credit or debit cards for purchases.

The second option—using non-compliant providers—is not much better. While these providers can facilitate transactions, they charge exorbitant fees, often bypassing regulatory frameworks to provide illegal payment solutions. These payments lack transparency, are prone to legal issues, and eventually get shut down by regulators. This leaves cannabis retailers back at square one, either unable to process payments or back to dealing with cash-only transactions, resulting in significant losses in both revenue and market share.


The Opportunity: A $33B Market, No Competition, and No Legal Payment Providers


This is where the opportunity lies. No legitimate provider has successfully penetrated the cannabis payment processing market with a fully compliant solution—until now. The market is in dire need of payment providers that can deliver:

  • Compliant Payment Systems: A trusted system that allows cannabis businesses to accept credit and debit cards, securely and within legal frameworks.
  • Transparent Transactions: Clear and traceable transactions that comply with banking regulations and ensure that businesses and consumers are not exposed to legal risks.
  • Fair and Reasonable Fees: Transaction fees that are reasonable and competitive, giving cannabis businesses a cost-effective alternative to high-fee, non-compliant solutions that exploit them.

The fact that no payment providers have been able to fill this gap has left a massive hole in the market—an opportunity waiting to be seized. In fact, this gap creates a $33 billion revenue opportunity for any provider capable of penetrating the cannabis industry with a compliant, scalable, and trusted solution.


Revenue Streams from Transaction Fees


For a provider who can successfully establish a compliant payment solution for the cannabis sector, the revenue potential from transaction fees is enormous. Here's why:

  • Massive Market Size: With $33 billion in annual transactional volume, even a small percentage of that market would generate significant revenue.
  • Low Competition: Since Visa and MasterCard refuse to service cannabis businesses, and the majority of providers are either illegal or non-compliant, the market is wide open for any legitimate player.
  • Recurring Transaction Fees: Payment processors earn a steady stream of revenue from each transaction made. In most industries, providers earn anywhere from 2% to 5% of the transaction amount in fees. Given the scale of the cannabis market, this can result in multi-million-dollar annual revenues for any provider able to capture even a small share of the market.
  • Rising Demand: As more states legalize cannabis and more consumers and businesses enter the market, the demand for compliant payment processing will only continue to rise, further increasing revenue potential for payment providers.

Example:


If just 1% of the $33 billion market is captured, that’s $330 million in transaction volume. With a typical transaction fee rate of 2-5%, this could translate to $6.6 million to $16.5 million in revenue for a payment processor capturing just 1% of the market volume.

But it doesn’t stop there. As more cannabis businesses turn to legitimate payment solutions and customer adoption increases, the market share and transaction volumes for providers can expand rapidly, driving even more revenue.


The Competitive Advantage: Securing a Bank Relationship


The key to unlocking this market—and the revenue potential—is the ability to secure a banking relationship. Without this, it is impossible to provide a compliant and scalable payment solution. By solving the banking issue, we’ve effectively set the stage for widespread adoption of our payment solution, including the adoption of our token payment option, which provides even greater cost savings and benefits for both retailers and consumers. The adoption of the token, once it goes live, further drives massive market growth.


Conclusion: The Perfect Storm for Investors


The cannabis industry is ripe for disruption. $33 billion in annual transactional volume is just the beginning, and it’s waiting for a provider to offer compliant, legal, and affordable payment solutions.

With no competition and the ability to secure a banking relationship, we’ve created the perfect environment to capitalize on this market, not only by solving the pain points for cannabis retailers but also by positioning ourselves as the first and only compliant payment provider in the industry.
