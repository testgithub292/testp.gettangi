Market Opportunity


The U.S. legal cannabis industry, generating over $33 billion annually, is undergoing rapid transformation as legalization expands across 38 states for medical use and 24 states plus the District of Columbia for recreational use. Driven by increasing consumer demand, policy shifts, and new market entrants like New York and New Jersey. California alone accounts for over $10 billion in sales, making it the world’s largest legal cannabis market.

Despite this growth, the industry faces significant operational and financial challenges due to federal restrictions classifying cannabis as a Schedule I substance:

  • Limited Banking Access: Most financial institutions avoid serving cannabis businesses due to federal penalties, forcing over 8,000 licensed dispensaries to operate primarily in cash. This increases risks of theft, fraud, and operational inefficiencies.
  • High Banking Fees: Dispensaries with banking access face exorbitant fees, ranging from $750 to $2,500 per month, straining profitability.
  • Cash-Only Transactions: Approximately 80% of cannabis transactions are cash-based, complicating payroll, tax payments, and customer convenience.
  • Competitive Pressure: Rising competition among dispensaries heightens the need for customer acquisition and retention strategies, with loyalty programs becoming critical.
  • E-Commerce and Payment Technology: Growing consumer preference for online shopping and mobile payments demands modern, compliant payment solutions.

With over 8,000 dispensaries and an estimated 60 million consumers, the cannabis industry represents a significant opportunity for innovative financial solutions. Tangi is uniquely positioned to address these challenges by offering a compliant, bank-backed payment ecosystem that replaces cash transactions, reduces fees, and enhances customer engagement. The platform targets a fragmented market of standalone dispensaries and emerging national brands, capitalizing on the industry’s consolidation and the urgent need for scalable, cashless payment infrastructure.
